# R2 Audit — Microcopy & Content Design

## Scope

Reviewed microcopy across home, compare, find-advisor, quiz/get-matched, advisor profile, advisor signup, pro join wizard, auth (login/signup), shortlist, community, reviews, quotes, and account pages. Searched for vague CTAs, generic error copy, jargon exposure, placeholder-as-label patterns, adviser/advisor spelling inconsistency, broker/platform terminology drift, and sentence/Title Case mixing in buttons.

## Notable

- **Broker vs. platform inconsistency (#1, #2, #13)** is the highest-impact nomenclature issue. The compare page, filter chips, result counts, and page titles all say "platforms", but the shortlist empty state, search fallback, and error boundaries still say "brokers". Beginners Googling "broker" land correctly, but once on-site they see a mixed signal about what the product is called.
- **Missing quiz confirmation screen (#3)** is the most consequential UX gap: users complete the highest-intent flow on the site (getting matched with an advisor) with no visible confirmation their details were actually sent. The `_confirmedAdvisorId` variable being prefixed with `_` signals this was deferred but not completed.
- **"Something went wrong" saturation (#4)**: 128 occurrences outside admin. Auth flows are the most damaging because users have no actionable next step — they can't tell if it's a network issue, a duplicate account, or a server error.
